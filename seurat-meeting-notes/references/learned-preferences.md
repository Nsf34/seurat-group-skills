### What to Exclude

- **Key Takeaways: no forward-looking or implication clauses** — bullets should state the fact cleanly; cut trailing context like "will be critical ahead of X" or "understanding this is important before Y" (seen in 1 session)

- **Key Takeaways: no attribution or opinion language** — strip "X surfaced that..." framing and remove bullets that express what someone *wants* rather than what was said or decided; state the core fact directly (seen in 1 session)
  - Example: "Mindy surfaced that Linda has a negative association with 'salon quality'" → "Linda has a negative association with 'salon quality'"
  - Bullets expressing desires/intentions (e.g., "Mark wants evangelical fervor from Linda") belong in Full Notes, not Key Takeaways

- **Next Steps: no attendee names in "[Client] to" bullets** — just state the action; do not name who on the client team is involved unless a specific individual owns a truly distinct deliverable (seen in 1 session)
  - Example: "Henkel to regroup internally on Feb. 17 with Linda, Yumi, and Carlos" → "Henkel to regroup internally on Feb. 17"

## Section-Specific Preferences

### Next Steps

- No individual attendee names in "[Client] to" bullets unless they own a distinct deliverable — see Content > What to Exclude above (seen in 1 session)

### Key Takeaways

- **Punctuation: use semicolons, not em dashes, to join clauses within a bullet** (seen in 1 session)
  - Example: "...directionally stronger — Elevated Expression..." → "...directionally stronger; Elevated Expression..."

- **Inline lists: use parentheses, not em dashes** — when listing examples mid-bullet, wrap in parentheses rather than surrounding with em dashes (seen in 1 session)
  - Example: "three spaces — Out the Door, Keep it Clean, Me Moment — and expects" → "three spaces (Out the Door, Keep it Clean, Me Moment) and expects"

- No forward-looking clauses, attribution framing, or opinion/desire language — see Content > What to Exclude above (seen in 1 session)
